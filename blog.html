How Aurora Got Me Walking Again (One Step at a Time)

I broke my back in three places. For months, the simplest things — sitting up, standing, reaching for a glass of water — felt like mountains. And through all of it, there was Aurora. She'd rest her head on my chest while I cried, nudge my hand when I'd stopped moving for too long, and wait by the front door every single day, tail wagging, believing I'd get better. She was right. This store is the proof. Everything here is something we tested together — on the days I could barely stand and the days I could finally walk her around the block again. She's not just my dog. She's the reason this place exists.

Walking Aurora After Dark — How We Stay Visible & Safe

After my back injury, I couldn't drive for months. Our evening walks became the highlight of my day — but when winter came and the sun set by 4pm, I realized how invisible a dark-furred dog can be on unlit streets. A close call with a car that didn't see us was enough. Now Aurora wears a lighted harness with LED strips and reflective stitching. I sleep better knowing drivers can spot her from a block away. Night walks went from stressful to magical — just us, the stars, and a soft glowing dog leading the way.

What Nobody Tells You About Recovering From a Back Injury With a Dog

The doctors told me to rest. They didn't mention that rest would be the hardest part — because Aurora needed walks, play, and attention. At first I felt guilty, like I was failing her. But then something shifted. The slow walks became my physiotherapy. Bending to attach her leash became my stretching. Getting up to feed her became my reason to move. She didn't care that I was slow. She just wanted to be with me. Studies show dog owners recover faster from injuries — but I didn't need a study. I had Aurora, nudging me forward one step at a time.
